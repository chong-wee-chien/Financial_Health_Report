# Risk And Simulation

## Purpose

Static reports tell the client what exists today. Simulation shows what could happen next.

This is one of the most important ways to make the product interactive.

## Risk View

Create a section called:

```text
What happens if...
```

Suggested scenarios:

- Income stops temporarily
- Major illness happens
- Hospitalisation happens
- Retirement happens earlier
- Inflation is higher
- Investment return is lower
- Family expenses increase

## Scenario card structure

Each card should show:

- Scenario name
- Current preparedness
- Risk level
- Short explanation
- Related numbers
- Suggested review action

## Simulation controls

Use sliders and toggles:

- Retirement age
- Monthly savings
- Investment return
- Inflation
- Income growth
- Expense growth

## UX principle

Do not make users fill in many fields upfront.

Start with defaults from the report, then let users adjust one variable at a time.

## Example interaction

User changes retirement age from 65 to 55.

The product updates:

- Projected assets
- Monthly retirement income estimate
- Funding gap
- Recommendation priority

## Design requirement

Show before and after clearly.

The client should immediately see the effect of a change.
