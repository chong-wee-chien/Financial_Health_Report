# Information Architecture

## Main structure

The product should not follow the old PDF page order. It should follow how a client thinks.

```text
Dashboard
├── Financial Health Score
├── Goals
├── Net Worth
├── Cash Flow
├── Protection
├── Investments
├── Retirement
├── Risk View
├── Recommendations
└── AI Coach
```

## Dashboard

Purpose: give the client a quick answer within 10 seconds.

Content:

- Overall score
- Status label
- Change since last review
- Health rings
- Top insight
- Top action
- Upcoming milestone

## Goals

Purpose: show whether the client is on track.

Content:

- Goal cards
- Target amount
- Current progress
- Timeline
- Funding gap
- Probability of success

## Net Worth

Purpose: show what the client owns, owes, and how it is allocated.

Content:

- Net worth number
- Asset allocation chart
- Liability summary
- Expandable categories

## Cash Flow

Purpose: show whether money is moving in the right direction.

Content:

- Monthly default view
- Annual toggle
- Income vs expense
- Savings amount
- Expense breakdown

## Protection

Purpose: show whether the client is covered for major life risks.

Content:

- Protection status cards
- Existing plans
- Premiums
- Gaps
- Claim guidance

## Retirement

Purpose: show future readiness.

Content:

- Projected value
- Retirement age slider
- Monthly income estimate
- Gap view

## Risk View

Purpose: make risk understandable through scenarios.

Content:

- Loss of income
- Major illness
- Early retirement
- Hospitalisation
- Inflation

## Recommendations

Purpose: create a clear review roadmap.

Content:

- Now
- Next
- Later
- Priority
- Impact
- Reason

## AI Coach

Purpose: let the client ask questions in plain language.

Example prompts:

- Why is my score lower?
- Can I retire earlier?
- What should I review first?
- Explain my CPF simply.
